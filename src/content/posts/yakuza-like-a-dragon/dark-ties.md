This has been the most controversial remake in the series, for a number of
reasons. The original game was already controversial enough. At least it has
some new content: a spinoff game containing the backstory of some antagonists.
But is is good?

No. Not really.

I'll keep this one short, because the game itself is short and there really
isn't much to talk about beyond my disappointment. Honestly, if I hadn't played
beyond the Steam refund window, I'd be strongly considering that right now.
After playing this and watching the set of retcons made to the remake of the
base game, I have no intention of playing the rest of the remake. Unfortunately,
to keep up a frankly insane release pace, this series sometimes has to release
some meaningless slop. This is one of those times. It's a bummer, because this
was the game that could have benefitted most from a good remake.

I'll start with the good. The fighting style of Mine, the protagonist of this
short story, is pretty fun. It's very fast and dynamic, mixing up elements of
other great styles across the series while maintaining a unique personality.
There are lots of sweet acrobatic moves, and it focuses on rewarding you for
aggressive defense such as parries and perfect dodges. You can also charge up a
special stance with much more heavy, brutal moves. It feels like the character
has been pushed beyond his breaking point and can no longer be _refined_ in his
fighting style. It's time to get dirty. Switching back and forth between these
styles keeps things fresh. There's something funny about the fact that this
style shares the most with Akiyama's. I guess being absurdly rich turns you into
a smug acrobat who loves to kick as often as possible.

The other good thing is that we get some extra time with Daigo. We get another
peek inside his mental state during these middle-games of the series. It's not
really much _character development,_ with how little screen time he gets, but it
does help fill in the gaps in his screen time in the base game. It also helps
foreshadow some of his mental state in the games to follow. Even with the
extremely limited time we get with him, this feels like the most important part
of this game's story. Sure, we technically get some other _content,_ but the
only important parts are Daigo's parts. And he's only in there for about 20
minutes.

I suppose I should clarify, _important and good._ Because there are retcons in
here too. Those are technically important. **Technically,** RGG could make
future games that tie into these story changes. I really, really hope they
don't. Because these are so ass.

Spoilers up, of course---but mostly because I have to talk about spoilers for
the original game to talk about what changed. There are some spoilers for the
rest of the series in here too, due to how these continuity changes connect
to the future.

||In the original, Mine is shown to be a deeply conflicted character. He has a
deep admiration for Daigo, and would do anything for him. At the same time, he
hungers for power, while trying to deny that hunger by masking it under other
pretenses. This manifests as being willing to kill Daigo in order to bring about
a future that fulfils Daigo's dreams Daigo himself could not bring about. Mine
saw it has his responsibility to be Daigo's shadow, willing to handle the dark
parts of their work so that Daigo wouldn't have to face them. As mentioned, he
was twisted enough that he would take that as far as murder of the man he
admires so much, to paradoxically make that man happy. In the original game,
this relationship is demonstrated, but we never learn how it came to be. Surely
the entire purpose of this game, which covers the story of how Daigo and Mine
met, would be to convey this information, right?||

||This does not happen. Instead, we get to see an extremely unsatisfying bonding
experience with another minor side antagonist. Most of the game focuses on this,
with only some occasional Mine content. Yet, the framing of the game continues
to insist that this is a game about Mine and Daigo. So it ends on an emotional
scene demonstrating why Mine would do anything for Daigo, and how he came to
admire him so much. So... half the picture, at least. It then entirely fails to
deliver on the other half of the conflict. We still don't get a good picture of
how Mine came to twist himself up so much that he would kill Daigo. We're just
shown that even on the day of the attempted murder, he still loved Daigo, and
still tried to kill him.||

||In the original, this all leads up to an emotional climax in which Mine ends
up aborting his plan, sacrificing himself to protect Daigo. It is the very peak
of his character arc, and the entire point of it. In this remake, he survives.
Fell off a 100-story skyscraper, but landed in a 2.5' bush. So he just walked it
off. No big deal. He does this corny-ass walk into the camera and announces that
now he's "another man who has erased his name," a reference to Kiryu faking his
own death to live in the shadows at the end of Yakuza 6. He then gives this
awful, bad fanfiction monologue to another antagonist that takes place entirely
for the audience. He goes on and on about how "Kiryu won, but where you see a
happy man, I see one living in torture and penance for his sins." while the
other antagonist answers only in prompting statements like "you really think
so?" and so on. This conversation sounds _nothing_ like either of these
characters do in any of the other places they exist in the series. It's just
somebody's random, entirely misplaced opinions about Kiryu inserted into these
characters' mouths.||

||All of this serves only to undermine some of the best parts of the original
game. It inserts unnecessary doubt and conflict into Kiryu's character arc that
doesn't need to be there yet. It entirely destroys Mine's original, emotional
character arc by taking all the bite out of his sacrifice. Maybe worse of all,
it has me worried that they are trying to set Mine up as some kind of anti-Kiryu
for the future. I can't see another reason for them to turn him into a man of
the shadows like this. I really hope they do not do this.||

I think another failure of this story is that it fails to actually tell us
anything new or recontextualize anything in an interesting thematic lens. We
already knew that Mine was a savvy and ruthless, if reckless, business man. We
already knew which family he came from. We already knew that he admired Daigo.
We already knew that Mine worked to become one of Daigo's favorites. This game
just uses all those points as an outline, and follows them rigidly. It doesn't
show how or why these things happened, just _that_ they happened. The most
important questions, which _should_ have been the focus of the entire game, were
glossed over and treated as unimportant pieces of information that there's no
time for. Meanwhile, we have time to dig into an obscure, absurd fighting arena
that has never appeared in the series before and never will again, and similar
_actually_ trivial things.

This story manages to actively harm the original while providing very little of
any benefit, and it does it while having terrible pacing. This side game took me
only 4 hours to complete. In there, I'd say maybe a half hour of story was
enjoyable and important to rest of the series in any way. The rest was all
padding. So. Much. Padding. There were several story locks, requiring you to
complete a certain amount of side content to continue. If they didn't have
those, the game would be less than an hour long. So at least 80% of my playtime
was just running around, getting in fights, and going on fetch quests.

To top it off, all these filler fights sucked. There were only a small handful
of enemy types, and some were quite frustrating to deal with. The enemy design
also got an annoying, artificial difficulty boost: They'd often gain an "aura"
that made them unable to be interrupted in any way. This removed any interesting
way to get around their defenses, and made combat very straightforward and
brainless whenever it happened. But, brainless in an annoying, hard way. It
forced a counter-attack-only playstyle.

Unfortunately, that's all I really have to say about this. My expectations were
low, and this failed to meet even those. 4/10. Do not recommend. I desperately
hope this game is forgotten even by its creators so that it can do no further
damage.
